Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish loved for its rich flavors. It consists of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried fluffy bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked with onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a blend of aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. Bhature is made from refined flour and yogurt, which makes it soft and puffy when fried. This dish is commonly enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or lunch and is often accompanied by pickles, onions, and a squeeze of lemon. Its combination of spicy, tangy, and savory flavors makes it a favorite comfort food across India.

Ingredients

Ingredients for Chole (Chickpea Curry)

  • Chickpeas (Kabuli chana) – 1 cup, soaked overnight
  • Onions – 2 medium, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes – 2 medium, pureed or chopped
  • Ginger – 1 inch piece, grated
  • Garlic – 3-4 cloves, minced
  • Green chilies – 1-2, chopped
  • Cumin seeds – 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder – ½ tsp
  • Amchur (dry mango powder) – ½ tsp (optional)
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil – 2-3 tbsp
  • Fresh coriander leaves – for garnish

Ingredients for Bhature (Fried Bread)

  • All-purpose flour (maida) – 2 cups
  • Yogurt – ¼ cup
  • Semolina (sooji/rava) – 1 tbsp (optional, for crispiness)
  • Baking powder – ½ tsp
  • Salt – ½ tsp
  • Water – as needed to knead the dough
  • Oil – for deep frying

Instructions

Instructions for Chole

  • Drain the soaked chickpeas and pressure cook them with water, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of turmeric until soft.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  • Add tomato puree, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Cook until oil separates from the masala.
  • Add the boiled chickpeas along with some water to make a gravy. Simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  • Add garam masala and amchur powder. Mix well.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Instructions for Bhature

  • In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, and salt.
  • Add yogurt and mix well. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  • Cover the dough and let it rest for 1–2 hours.
  • Divide the dough into small balls.
  • Roll each ball into a round disc using a rolling pin.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry each bhatura until it puffs up and turns golden brown on both sides.
  • Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
