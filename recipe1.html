Samosa

A samosa is a popular Indian snack made of a thin, crispy pastry filled with a savory mixture, usually consisting of spiced potatoes, peas, onions, and sometimes meat. It is triangular in shape and deep-fried until golden brown. Often enjoyed as a tea-time snack or street food, samosas are loved for their crunchy exterior and flavorful filling, and are typically served with tangy chutneys like tamarind or mint

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida) - for the dough
  • Water - to knead the dough
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - for the dough and frying
  • Potatoes - boiled and mashed, for the filling
  • Green peas - for the filling
  • Onions - finely chopped (optional)
  • Green chilies - finely chopped (optional)
  • Ginger - grated or minced
  • Cumin seeds - for flavor
  • Coriander powder - for seasoning
  • Garam masala - for flavor
  • Red chili powder - for spice
  • Tamarind chutney or mint chutney - for serving

Instructions

  • Prepare the dough: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, salt, and 2 tbsp oil. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and set aside for 20-30 minutes.
  • Prepare the filling: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger. Saut√© until onions turn soft.
  • Add boiled and mashed potatoes, green peas, coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Cook the mixture for 5-7 minutes on low heat. Turn off and let it cool.
  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  • Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle. Cut it in half to form two semi-circles.
  • Take one semi-circle and form a cone by folding the straight edge and sealing it with water.
  • Fill the cone with the potato-pea mixture.
  • Seal the open edges of the cone carefully using water.
  • Repeat the process for all dough portions.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Test by dropping a small piece of dough; it should rise slowly and bubble.
  • Fry samosas on medium heat until golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  • Remove and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Serve hot with tamarind or mint chutney.
