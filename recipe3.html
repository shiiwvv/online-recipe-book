Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish known for its thin, crispy texture and savory taste. It is a type of fermented pancake made from a batter of rice and urad dal (split black gram). Dosas are typically served hot and can be plain or stuffed with fillings such as spiced mashed potatoes (masala dosa). They are often accompanied by coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar (a tangy lentil soup). Loved for its light and crispy texture, dosa is a versatile dish enjoyed as breakfast, lunch, or a snack across India and around the world.

Ingredients

  • Raw rice – 1 cup (regular rice or parboiled rice)
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – ¼ cup
  • Chana dal (optional) – 1 tbsp, for extra crispiness
  • Fenugreek seeds – ½ tsp (helps in fermentation)
  • Water – as needed for soaking and grinding
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil or ghee – for cooking the dosa on the pan

Instructions

  • Rinse the rice, urad dal, and chana dal separately under running water until water runs clear.
  • Soak the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds in water separately for 4–6 hours or overnight.
  • Drain the water from urad dal and chana dal. Grind them to a smooth, fluffy batter using some water as needed.
  • Drain the rice and grind it into a slightly coarse batter using water as needed.
  • Mix both batters together in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well.
  • Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8–12 hours or overnight, until it rises and becomes slightly bubbly.
  • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron pan over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it outward in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
  • Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
  • Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp. Flip only if you prefer both sides cooked.
  • Remove from the pan and serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or sambar.
  • Repeat the process for the remaining batter.
